# Syllabus: Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics

**Course Title:** Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics
**Target Audience:** AI engineers transitioning into robotics, robotics students with AI background, technical educators designing humanoid robotics curricula.
**Course Duration:** 13 Weeks (Assumed Quarter-length Course)
**Prerequisites:** Proficiency in Python, conceptual understanding of AI/ML.

**Course Objectives:**
Upon successful completion of this course, students will be able to:
*   Explain Physical AI and embodied intelligence.
*   Build and simulate a humanoid robot control pipeline.
*   Connect LLMs to real robotic action.
*   Design a voice-controlled autonomous humanoid system.

**Weekly Breakdown:**

*   **Week 1-2: Part 1 - Foundations of Physical AI & Embodied Intelligence**
    *   **Chapter 1:** Introduction to Physical AI
        *   Define Physical AI and its significance.
        *   Describe key challenges in humanoid robotics.
    *   **Chapter 2:** Embodied Intelligence
        *   Explain the embodiment thesis.
        *   Discuss how sensors and actuators influence robot intelligence.

*   **Week 3-4: Part 2 - The Robotic Nervous System (ROS 2)**
    *   **Chapter 3:** ROS 2 Architecture
        *   Explain the ROS 2 graph and core concepts.
        *   Use ROS 2 CLI to inspect a running system.
    *   **Chapter 4:** Building a ROS 2 System
        *   Create ROS 2 workspace and packages.
        *   Write simple publisher/subscriber nodes in Python.

*   **Week 5-6: Part 3 - Digital Twins & Physics Simulation**
    *   **Chapter 5:** Simulation-First Development
        *   Explain benefits of simulation-first approach.
        *   Describe simulation-to-real workflow.
    *   **Chapter 6:** Simulating Robots with Gazebo and Unity
        *   Create URDF model of a simple robot.
        *   Simulate a robot in Gazebo and Unity.

*   **Week 7-8: Part 4 - AI Perception, Learning, and Control**
    *   **Chapter 7:** Perception with NVIDIA Isaac
        *   Set up and use NVIDIA Isaac Sim for perception.
        *   Train a simple perception model using synthetic data.
    *   **Chapter 8:** Control Pipelines
        *   Explain robotic control loop components.
        *   Use MoveIt 2 for simple motion planning.

*   **Week 9-10: Part 5 - Vision–Language–Action Systems**
    *   **Chapter 9:** Connecting LLMs to ROS 2
        *   Create ROS 2 node for LLM communication.
        *   Design prompts for structured robotic commands.
    *   **Chapter 10:** Building a Vision-Language-Action System
        *   Integrate vision model into ROS 2.
        *   Build end-to-end VLA system.

*   **Week 11-12: Part 6 - Capstone: Autonomous Conversational Humanoid**
    *   **Chapter 11:** The Capstone Project
        *   Design and build autonomous conversational humanoid in simulation.
        *   Integrate concepts into a working system.
    *   **Chapter 12:** Future Directions
        *   Identify open research problems.
        *   Find resources for continued learning.

*   **Week 13: Final Project Presentation/Review**
